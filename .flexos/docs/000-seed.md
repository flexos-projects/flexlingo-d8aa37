# FlexLingo

## 1. Vision

You are sitting at a dinner table in Berlin. There are four people, and between you, you speak three different native languages. The conversation is halting. Someone pulls out their phone, opens a translation app, types a sentence, shows the screen, and waits. The moment dies. The laughter fades. 

Or you are trying to learn Spanish. You download an app with a green owl. You tap words on a screen for twenty minutes a day. You learn how to say "the boy eats an apple." Six months later, you arrive in Madrid, a barista asks you a simple question, and you freeze. You know vocabulary, but you don't know how to *converse*.

FlexLingo destroys both of these problems by fundamentally reimagining what a chat application can be. It is not a translation utility, and it is not a flashcard app. It is a real-time communication platform where language disappears as a barrier and becomes a seamless, invisible bridge.

The core of the product is the "Flex Translation." When you send a message in FlexLingo, the recipient doesn't just get a crude translation. They get a message bubble with three distinct layers: the **Original** text exactly as typed or spoken, a **Cleaned** version that corrects grammar and slang without losing the speaker's voice, and a meaning-first **Translated** version in their native language. It shifts the burden of comprehension from the humans to the system. 

FlexLingo is for the international friend group that wants a group chat where everyone types in their native language and reads in their native language. It is for the language learner who wants to chat with a custom AI partner—a "Spanish Grandmother" or a "Japanese Gamer"—who remembers their life, corrects their grammar gently, and initiates conversations proactively. It is for the traveler who needs to drop their phone on a table and have it transcribe and translate a live, multi-person conversation in real-time.

This is the exact right moment for this product. Large Language Models are finally fast enough, cheap enough, and context-aware enough to provide instant, culturally accurate translations and text-to-speech audio without breaking the natural flow of a chat interface. We are moving past word-for-word translation into intent-for-intent communication.

FlexLingo is not a corporate enterprise tool. It is vibrant, warm, and distinctly human. It borrows the addictive, habit-forming mechanics of social apps—like daily streaks and fire emojis—and applies them to actual, meaningful communication. It is the end of language barriers, wrapped in an interface that feels as familiar as texting your best friend.

## 2. Features

### The Flex Translation Bubble
The defining feature of the platform. Every single message—whether text or transcribed audio—is processed into three distinct layers, accessible via inline tabs within the chat bubble. "Original" shows exactly what was sent. "Cleaned" shows the grammatically perfected version of the original language (an incredible learning tool). "Translated" shows the meaning-first localization in the reader's native language. No jumping to a new screen. No long-pressing to "See Translation." It is native to the reading experience.

### Custom AI Language Partners
Users don't just chat with a generic "AI." They create specific personas. You describe the partner you want—"A friendly 20-something from Paris who loves art and coffee"—and the system generates a name, a bio, a visual avatar, and a deep system prompt. These bots stick to their native language, gently correct your grammar, and feel entirely real. They don't lecture; they text.

### Proactive Bot Storylines
The feature that drives unprecedented retention. Bots don't just sit idle waiting for you to practice. Once a week, the system generates a "life event" for the bot—going to a football match, trying a new restaurant, having a busy day at work. If you haven't messaged them in a while, the bot will text *you* proactively to tell you about their day. It creates the illusion of a real friend with an independent life, transforming a "study session" into a genuine catch-up.

### Universal Group Chats
Create a group chat with a mix of French, German, and English speakers. Each person sets their preferred language. When the German speaker types a message, it is instantly translated and delivered to the French speaker in French, and the English speaker in English. The group chat maintains a language register for every participant, executing parallel translations seamlessly.

### Live Translation Sessions
For in-person interactions. A host starts a "Live" session and generates a shareable code or QR. Guests join on their own phones, selecting their language. The app continuously listens, detects when someone stops speaking, transcribes the audio, and broadcasts the translated text to everyone's screen like a scrolling movie subtitle. Perfect for international dinners, meetups, or spontaneous travel interactions.

### Voice & Pronunciation Engine
Hold to record a voice message. The system transcribes it verbatim (warts and all), cleans it, translates it, and delivers it. On the receiving end, users can play back the text-to-speech (TTS) of any translated message using 10 different high-quality, emotionally resonant voices. They can slow the audio down to 0.5x to catch the pronunciation or speed it up.

### The Onboarding Hook (Guest Invites)
Growth is built directly into the core loop. When a user invites a friend to chat, the friend clicks a link and drops immediately into the conversation. They don't need to create an account or download an app. They immediately see the magic of the Flex Translation on the very first message. Once they experience the "aha" moment, they are prompted to save their account with an email and password to keep replying.

### Learning Streaks & Accountability
Language learning requires consistency. FlexLingo uses Snapchat-style daily streaks. Send at least one message per day in your target language to keep the fire emoji alive. The system validates that the message is actually in the target language (you can't just type "hi" in English to save your Spanish streak). Premium users get "Streak Freezes" to protect their progress on busy days.

### Tutor & Notebook
A dedicated space for structured learning. A conversational AI tutor helps explain complex grammar rules, cultural idioms, or vocabulary. When the tutor teaches a new phrase, users can seamlessly save it to their personal "Notebook," which automatically generates flashcards with TTS audio for spaced repetition practice.

## 3. Pages

The application is built as a Progressive Web App (PWA), meaning it behaves like a native mobile app. The navigation is anchored by a persistent bottom tab bar on mobile, and a collapsible sidebar on desktop.

The journey starts at the **Landing Page**, a vibrant, scrolling introduction to the product that features a live, interactive demo of the Flex Translation bubble. From there, users move to **Auth**, a frictionless email/password or magic link login. First-time users flow through **Onboarding**, where they select their native language, add a display name, and pick the languages they want to learn.

Once inside, the default view is the **Chats** list. This resembles standard messaging apps (WhatsApp, Telegram) but with powerful filtering pills at the top: All, People, AI Partners, Groups. Each list item shows the avatar, the chat name, a snippet of the last message, and a blazing fire emoji if a streak is active.

Tapping the floating action button leads to the **New Chat** menu, presenting four distinct paths: Invite Someone, Start Group Chat, Create AI Partner, or Live Translation. 

Choosing "Create AI Partner" takes the user into a magical, wizard-like flow. The **Bot Creator** page asks for a simple text prompt ("A friendly baker from Rome"). The system spins up, showing a loading state while Gemini generates the bot's persona, system instructions, and a custom avatar. The user reviews the generated profile and hits "Start Chatting."

The core of the app is the **Universal Chat View**. This single, highly dynamic page adapts to whether you are talking to a human, a bot, a group, or participating in a live session. The header shows the recipient's name and a flag representing their language. The message area is a vertical scroll of messages, each featuring the signature Flex Translation tabs. The input area handles text, image attachments, and a hold-to-record microphone button for voice notes.

The **Tutor** tab in the main navigation opens a split experience: Chat and Notebook. The Chat side is a conversation with a strict, helpful AI teacher. The Notebook side houses saved phrases and launches a full-screen, swipeable **Flashcard** modal with TTS audio playback.

The **Contacts** page handles friend requests and user search via username, generating personal QR codes for easy sharing. Finally, the **Settings** page allows users to manage their subscription (Free, Flex+, Flex Pro), adjust voice autoplay preferences, toggle dark mode, and manage their native/learning languages.

## 4. Data

The data architecture is optimized for real-time syncing across devices. It uses a NoSQL document model where chats and messages are the central entities.

A **User** represents a human interacting with the system. They have a native language, an array of learning languages, and preferences. Crucially, they have a `friendCount` and a `friends` array to manage their social graph.

A **Chat** is the container for a conversation. Because a chat can be 1:1, a Group, an AI Bot, or a Live Session, the Chat document is polymorphic. It stores an aggregate of its participants' languages so the system immediately knows which translations need to be generated for any incoming message. It also tracks the `streakCount` and the `lastMessageAt` timestamp.

A **Message** is the heaviest, most critical document. It is stored in a subcollection under the Chat. A single message document contains the `originalText`, the `originalLanguage`, the `formattedText` (which is the grammar-cleaned version), and a `translations` map containing the translated text for every target language required by the chat participants. If the message was spoken, it also stores the `voiceUrl`.

A **CustomBot** is an independent entity created by a user. It has a name, a native language, personality traits, and a massive `systemPrompt` that dictates its behavior. It also stores a `memory` array (things it has learned about the user) and a `currentStoryline` (its generated weekly life event used for proactive messaging).

A **Notebook Entry** belongs to a user and stores a specific phrase, its translation, and the language code, allowing it to be queried and rendered as a flashcard.

## 5. Flows

### The Magic Invite (Guest to User)
You are organizing a trip to Tokyo with three friends. Two speak English, one speaks Japanese. You create a Group Chat in FlexLingo and send an invite link to the group text. 

Kenji, who doesn't have the app, taps the link. His browser opens to a beautiful landing screen: "You're invited to Tokyo Trip! Join the conversation in your language." He selects Japanese and taps Join. He is instantly dropped into the live chat as a Guest. He sees your English messages already translated perfectly into Japanese. He taps a message and sees the Original English tab. He hits the microphone icon, speaks in Japanese, and sends it. On your phone, his audio is transcribed and translated into English instantly. 

Kenji is blown away. When he tries to leave, a gentle prompt appears: "Secure your account so you don't lose this conversation." He enters an email and password, his Guest account is upgraded to a permanent User, and FlexLingo just acquired a new, highly engaged user with zero friction.

### Breathing Life into an AI Partner
You are learning French. You go to the Bots tab and tap "Create AI Friend." The app asks what kind of person you want to talk to. You type: "A moody 20-something barista in Paris who loves cinema and hates tourists." 

You hit generate. Ten seconds later, the screen reveals "Mathieu," complete with an illustrated avatar of a guy in a beanie, a bio matching your prompt, and a native language of French. You tap "Start Chatting." 

Mathieu sends the first message: "Salut. Qu'est-ce que tu veux ?" (Hi. What do you want?). You try to reply in French: "Je un café s'il vous plaît." You hit send. The message bubble appears. You tap the "Cleaned" tab on your own message, and the AI gently shows you that it should be "Je *voudrais* un café s'il vous plaît." You just learned a grammar rule naturally, in context, while arguing with a simulated Parisian barista.

### The Daily Streak Save
It is 9:00 PM on a Thursday. You are exhausted. Your phone buzzes with a push notification: "Your streak with Maria expires in 3 hours! 🔥" 

You open FlexLingo. Maria (your AI Spanish partner) had sent a message earlier that day: "Fui a un restaurante nuevo hoy, ¡la paella estaba increíble!" (I went to a new restaurant today, the paella was incredible! — generated by her weekly storyline). 

You don't have the energy to type. You hold the microphone button and say, in your best broken Spanish, "Eso suena delicioso. ¿Qué más comiste?" (That sounds delicious. What else did you eat?). The app transcribes it, checks that it is actually Spanish, sends the message, and a massive celebratory animation fills the screen. The fire emoji ticks up to 48. Your streak is saved, and you practiced speaking Spanish today.

## 6. Design

FlexLingo feels like a premium, modern consumer messaging app, but with an educational superpower hidden inside the UI. The visual language is vibrant and energetic, anchored by an Emerald Green primary color that signifies growth, correctness, and progression. 

The interface disappears to let the conversation shine. Chat bubbles are generously rounded. Avatars are large and expressive. Flag emojis serve as crucial, instantly recognizable metadata on every message, grounding the user in the multilingual reality of the platform.

The defining UI component is the **Flex Translation Tab Bar**. Attached seamlessly to the bottom of the message content within the bubble, it is a row of three pill-shaped buttons: Original, Cleaned, Translated. It must feel absolutely native—no janky height jumps when switching tabs, just a smooth crossfade of the text above it. 

Dark mode is a first-class citizen. In dark mode, the app transforms into a sleek, high-contrast environment. The deep charcoal backgrounds (`oklch(18% 0.01 260)`) make the Emerald Green sent-message bubbles pop dramatically, while received messages sit comfortably in elevated surface colors. 

Typography plays a distinct dual role. `Quicksand` provides a friendly, rounded, slightly playful voice for headers, empty states, and marketing copy. `Inter` does the heavy lifting for the actual message text, ensuring legibility across 37 different languages, alphabets, and character sets.

### Component Patterns

**Message Bubble**
The sent bubble uses `--color-primary` background with white text, utilizing `--radius-bubble-sent` (sharp bottom-right corner). The received bubble uses `--color-surface` with `--color-text` and a subtle `--shadow-sm`, utilizing `--radius-bubble-recv` (sharp bottom-left corner). Inside the bubble, the Flex Translation tabs sit at the bottom. The active tab is indicated by a semi-transparent white background (sent) or `--color-primary` background (received).

**Chat Input**

## 7. Technical

FlexLingo is built for speed, real-time sync, and rapid iteration. The architecture is a modern serverless SPA. 

The frontend is **Nuxt 4** operating entirely in SPA mode. This ensures that once the PWA is installed or the site is loaded, navigating between chats is instantaneous. The heavy lifting of rendering happens on the client, minimizing server round-trips for UI updates. 

The database is **Cloud Firestore**, chosen specifically for its `onSnapshot` real-time listeners. In a chat application, polling is unacceptable. Firestore allows messages to appear on the recipient's screen the millisecond they are written to the database. It also handles offline caching beautifully, allowing users to read past messages even when they drop into a subway tunnel.

The AI engine is **Google Gemini** (`gemini-2.5-flash`), accessed securely via server-side API routes (`/api/ai/...`). Gemini was selected because it is incredibly fast and uniquely multi-modal natively handling text, image generation (for bot avatars), and audio transcription/TTS without needing to stitch together three different services (e.g., OpenAI + ElevenLabs + Whisper). The "Flex Translation" DAG pipeline runs entirely in a Nuxt Nitro endpoint: it takes an incoming message, checks the chat document to see which languages are required, transcribes if necessary, cleans the native text, and parallel-translates to all required target languages in a single LLM call, returning structured JSON.

## 8. Content

FlexLingo's content challenge is primarily about setting the right tone for the AI interactions and ensuring the marketing site clearly communicates a complex paradigm (the Flex Translation).

**What We Have:**
The codebase already contains a robust taxonomy of 37 supported languages, complete with correct flag emojis, native language names, and localized guest display names (e.g., "A Friendly French Speaker" translates to "Un Francophone Amical"). We also have a curated list of 30 Gemini TTS voices categorized by emotional register (e.g., "Zephyr: Bright, Female"). The system prompt engineering for the bots is highly developed, ensuring they stay in character and respect language boundaries.

**What We Need:**
The app requires a suite of default AI Bot templates. While users can create their own, we need 10-15 pre-configured personas covering the most popular languages (e.g., "Diego the Madrid Chef," "Yuki the Tokyo Tour Guide") to reduce friction for new users.

We also need to write the weekly storyline generation prompts. This is a critical retention feature. The AI needs a robust template to generate mundane but highly conversational "life events" (e.g., "I went to the cinema last night but fell asleep") that the bots can use to proactively text the users.

Finally, the empty states need warm, illustrated microcopy. A blank chat screen is intimidating; it needs to be filled with localized conversation starters.

## 9. Downstream Alerts
- ALERT: [Firestore Read Costs] → The Group Chat "historic translation on demand" flow needs careful indexing, or a very active 10-person group chat could cause read spikes. → architectural review
- ALERT: [Audio Latency] → Web Audio API capture to Firebase Storage to Gemini Transcribe introduces a minimum ~1.5s latency. The UI must aggressively use optimistic "Processing..." states to hide this from the user. → UX review
- ALERT: [PWA Push Notifications] → iOS support for Web Push is historically finicky and requires the PWA to be installed to the home screen. The onboarding flow needs to explicitly guide iOS users to "Add to Home Screen" before requesting notification permissions. → conversation agenda
- ALERT: [Guest Auth Upgrade] → The `linkWithCredential` flow from anonymous to permanent accounts can fail if the user's email is already registered. Needs graceful error handling and merging strategy. → technical review
